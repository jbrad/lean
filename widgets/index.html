Widgets

Giving style and personality to your sidebars

Drag and drop the widget into one of the widget areas such as the Header or Sidebar.

Personal Image

Description

The personal image widget provides the ability to configure a picture, typically a photograph of yourself or your brand mark, and a short biographical description.

Settings

Image

To add an image to the widget, click the “Add Image” silhouette and you will be prompted to upload an image from your computer (or select from the media library).

Bio

Enter text or HTML into the bio field and then click save.

Social Influence

Description

The social influence widget was designed to help you establish credibility with your growing readership. It integrates with both Twitter and Facebook Fan Pages.

If you are in the process of building your online readership, this is an important visual indicator for both you and your audience. You can create and measure goals based on this number. Your audience will be able to identify if you are a new, up-and-coming publisher and help you get the word out. Or, if you have a large number of followers, then new readers will be able to ascertain a degree of credibility with your work.

The widget makes a call to Twitter & Facebook once a day to fetch current numbers, then it uses a saved version of these values for 24 hours. This is due to restrictions imposed by Twitter and Facebook.

Settings

Twitter

Enter your twitter username, do not include the @ sign prefix.

Facebook

Enter the name of your Facebook fan page account.

Display

Select one of the following settings:

  • Individual Count – Shows the individual follower counts for each social network.
  • Total Influence – Shows one number representing the sum of all social network follower counts.
  • Both – Shows the Total Influence sum and the individual follower counts for configured social networks.

Activity Tabs

Description

The Activity Tabs widget was designed to keep visitors on your site by creating a list of popular, active, or topical posts. Visitors often enter a site via a link to a single post, and not through the home page. In cases like this, it is considered good practice to provide a strategic list of additional posts to entice readers to stick around and read more articles.

Settings

Recent Posts

Show up to 10 of the latest published posts.

Most Commented Posts

Show up to 10 posts ordered by sequently by most comments to least comments.

Most Recent Comments

Show up to 10 comments order sequently by the most recent to the least recent.

Most Used Tags

Show up to 40 of the most used tags, in blocks of 10.

NOTE: If a tab is configured to show ’0′ items, the entire tab will be hidden from view for site visitors.

Google Custom Search

Description

This widget helps you configure a search box on your site that is powered by Google instead of WordPress. It requires you to have a Google Custom Search Account (free). If you do not already have one, see the instructions below.

You will want to do this for two reasons. First, it is important that readers can find what they are looking for, and nobody does searching better than Google. Second, by setting up a Google Custom Search account, you are making an explicit request to have Google add every page (and post!) added to it’s search engine database. It’s like setting up a daily schedule for Google to look at your site for new content.

In short, it’s an SEO technique.

Settings

The only required setting is your unique Search Engine ID provided by Google.

Does Your Widget Look Different?

To find the embed code visit the “Get Code” menu item on Google’s site.

Important

In order for this widget to work, it must create and manage it’s own page in the Pages section of your site. This page will be named Search Results and use the permalink search-results.

If you already have a page on your site that uses the permalink search-results, you will be required to rename the permalink or delete the existing page in order for the widget to work.

Setting Up a Custom Search Engine on Google

  1. Go to Google Custom Search.
  2. Create a new search engine.
  3. Copy the Search Engine Unique ID code from the Basics section.

Advertisement 300x250

Description

DIMENSIONS: 300 pixels wide by 250 pixels tall.

Add a single image to you sidebar, and select the destination page, where users are taken, when it is clicked. This works great for advertisements, but it is also useful to drive traffic to other parts of your own website.

Instead of putting blog post categories in your top navigation, why not create unique images for each one, and put them in your sidebar? Get creative!

NOTE If your image is not sized correctly before you upload, visual distortion will occur.

Settings

Upload

Click the Upload area then drag and drop your image onto the target area.

Link URL

This is the destination for visitors who click the advertisement image. It can by an on-site or off-site link.
